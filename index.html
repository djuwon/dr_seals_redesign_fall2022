Dr. Cheryl Seals is an associate professor in Auburn University's Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering. Her research areas of expertise are human computer interaction, user interface design, usability evaluation and educational gaming technologies. Seals also works with outreach initiatives to improve computer science education at all levels. The programs are focused on increasing the computing pipeline by getting students interested in STEM disciplines and future technology careers.

Courses
Comp 7620: Human Computer Interaction
Theory and practice of human computer interaction; designing, implementing and evaluating human computer interation.
Comp 6620: User Interface Design and Evaluation
Theory and practice of designing interfaces for interactive systems, usability engineering techniques; implementing and evaluating interfaces.
Comp 4730 : Computer Ethics
Computer Ethics provides students a foundation to understand the application of ethical principles to practical problems encountered by the practicing computer professional.
Comp 4640: Intelligent and Interactive Systems
This course combines classical Artificial Intelligence and Interactive Systems. Some of the topics addressed are intelligent behavior in artifacts, perception, reasoning, learning, and acting in complex environments. Interactive Systems will explore systems that are intelligent and the interaction between humans and computers.
Comp 4970: Introduction to Computer Game Design
Game Development provides an introduction to current and proposed techniques for electronic game design and programming. Topics will include graphics game engines, motion generation, behavioral control for autonomous characters, interaction structure, and social and interface issues of multi-user play.
Comp 7930: Machine Intelligence
This course will provide a broad overview of existing methods for machine learning and an introduction to self-organizing systems in general. By the end of the semester you should be familiar with the central concepts and most commonly used methods in this field.
Comp 7970: Game Design and Development
This course will provide an introduction to current and future techniques for electronic game design and programming. Topics will include graphics game engines, motion generation, behavioral control for autonomous characters, interaction structure, and social and interface issues of multi-user play.
